FAQ

Questions we actually get.

Where does the data come from? +
We aggregate data from Reddit (multiple subreddits per category), Twitter/X, Indie Hackers, G2 and Capterra reviews, and niche forums relevant to e-commerce and SaaS. All sources are public. We scan 2.3M+ posts per month and filter down to the highest-signal opportunities.
How is this different from just browsing Reddit myself? +
Three things. Volume — we cover dozens of sources simultaneously, not just the subreddits you know. Filtering — raw complaints aren't ideas; we add complaint volume, pain severity scoring, and an opportunity brief for each. Time — a full month of research delivered in 5 minutes of reading.
Are these ideas exclusive to subscribers? +
No, and we're upfront about that. The problems in the report are real, public complaints — anyone could find them with enough time. What we provide is the curation, analysis, and framing. Execution is still entirely up to you.
What format is the report? +
A professionally formatted PDF, delivered to your inbox on the 1st of each month. No dashboard to log into, no app to download. Works on any device. Pro and Team subscribers also receive weekly trend alerts via email.
Can I cancel anytime? +
Yes. Cancel before your next billing date and you won't be charged again. You keep access to every report you've already received. No cancellation fees, no awkward retention flows.
I'm not technical. Is this still useful for me? +
Absolutely. The report is written for anyone who wants to build a product — technical or not. The opportunity briefs include pricing suggestions, target audiences, and scope descriptions that are useful whether you're hiring a developer, using no-code tools, or writing code yourself.
